Odyssey VR

An abandoned deep-space installation where the lights never fully died — and neither did whatever was left inside.

Year2025
RoleSolo Environment Artist
EngineUnity
PlatformVR
The Vision

A station that
refuses to die

✦

Odyssey VR was built around a single atmospheric premise: what does it feel like to be the first person to walk these corridors in decades? The station has been dark for years — power rerouted, systems offline — but the emergency lighting still pulses a slow, arterial red. It doesn't guide you. It just reminds you the ship is still alive.

Every space was designed with VR presence in mind. Scale is precise and deliberate — the ceiling is low enough to feel claustrophobic, the corridors wide enough to navigate but tight enough to disorient. Rust bleeds through bolted plating in layers, each surface telling a different chapter of the station's decay. The industrial modules, ladders, and crawlspaces reward exploration without feeling manufactured.

The lighting strategy contrasted two distinct emotional states: the cold, clinical fluorescents of the intact corridors and the collapsed crimson emergency mode of the breached sections. Transitioning between them without a cutscene — just by walking through a doorway — became the core of the spatial storytelling.
